## 1. Definitions and Scope

### 1.1 Core Definitions

- **Personal Information**: Refers to information recorded electronically or otherwise that can identify a specific natural person, either alone or in combination with other information, including usernames, email addresses, password encryption values, registration time, avatar URLs, and operation log records mentioned in this policy.
- **Sensitive Personal Information**: Refers to personal information that, if leaked or illegally used, could easily lead to infringement upon the personal dignity of a natural person or endanger their personal or property safety. This studio **does not collect or process** any sensitive personal information.
- **Third-Party Image Hosting:** Refers to publicly available image hosting service platforms (such as SM.MS, Imgur, GitHub, etc.) that users independently select and upload images to, independent of this studio. This studio only receives the URL string returned by these platforms.
- **Cloudflare:** Refers to the global CDN, DDoS protection, HTTPS encrypted transmission, and R2 object storage services provided by Cloudflare, Inc. This entity is the technical infrastructure provider for this service and, as an **independent data controller**, collects the network interaction information required for its service.
- **Microsoft Account Login:** Refers to a third-party identity authentication service implemented through the Microsoft OAuth 2.0 protocol, allowing users to log in or bind their Microsoft accounts to this website.
- **OAuth 2.0 Authorization Service:** Refers to the OAuth 2.0 authorization service provided by this website, allowing users to authorize third-party applications to use their website accounts.
- **QR Code Login:** Refers to a cross-device identity authentication service implemented by scanning a QR code, allowing users to quickly log in on a computer via a mobile device by scanning a QR code.

### 1.2 Scope of Application

This policy applies to how we process your personal information when you access, browse, or use the Nebula Studios website (domains nebulastudios.top, nbls.top, and their subdomains). This policy does not apply to any third-party platforms or other websites you access through this website.

## 2. Scenarios and Purposes of Personal Information Collection

We strictly adhere to the "minimum necessary" principle stipulated in the Personal Information Protection Law, collecting information only to the extent necessary to achieve the core functions of the user account. All collection is premised on the user's **express consent** or **necessary for contractual performance**.

### 2.1 Account Registration Information Collection

**Collection Content**: Your **username**, **email address**, **password** (the original password is transmitted via an HTTPS encrypted channel on the client side; the server only stores the irreversible Argon2id algorithm encrypted hash value, which cannot be restored to plaintext), and **registration time** (server timestamp accurate to the second) that you actively fill in on the registration form.

**Collection Method:** Users submit their information via a form on the registration page. All fields are mandatory; failure to fill in any field will prevent registration from being completed.

**Collection Purpose:** To establish a unique user identifier and provide basic functions such as account login, identity authentication, and service notifications.

**Legal Basis:** Article 13, Paragraph 1 of the Personal Information Protection Law—Obtaining the individual's **explicit consent**.

**Storage Period:** From the date of account creation to the date of account cancellation. Upon account cancellation, the above information will be immediately deleted from the main database. Backup data will be automatically deleted within 30 days during backup rotation and cannot be recovered after deletion.

### 2.2 Avatar Display Function

This website supports two avatar sources, with different processing methods:

#### 2.2.1 User-Custom Avatar (Third-Party Image Hosting URL)

**Collection Content:** **Avatar URL string** obtained by the user from a third-party image hosting service. For this type of avatar, our studio **does not accept, store, or process** the original image file itself.

**Processing Flow**:
1. Users upload their profile picture to their chosen third-party image hosting platform and obtain the publicly accessible URL.
2. Users copy and paste the URL into the profile picture field on their account settings page on this website.
3. This website only stores the URL as a text string and displays it on the user's profile page using the &lt;img&gt; tag.
4. The actual transmission, loading, and caching of the image are handled directly between the user's browser and the third-party image hosting platform. Our studio's servers do not intervene in any part of the image data transmission process.

**Legal Explanation**: Your act of uploading an image to a third-party image hosting platform constitutes a **separate legal relationship between you and the image hosting service provider**. Our studio only acts as a "passive recipient" of the URL you actively provide. You are responsible for ensuring that you have obtained the legal right to use the image and that the image content does not infringe on the rights of others. Any legal disputes arising from the image content shall be borne by you and the image hosting service provider; our studio has no obligation to review such disputes.

#### 2.2.2 Microsoft Account Avatar

**Content Collected**: When you log in or link your Microsoft account, we obtain your avatar image data (base64 format) from Microsoft.

**Processing Flow**:
1. After successful Microsoft OAuth authorization, we obtain your avatar image (base64 encoded) from the Microsoft Graph API.
2. We convert the base64 image data to **WebP format** to optimize storage and loading performance.
3. The converted image file is uploaded to the **Cloudflare R2** object storage service.
4. We store the access URL of this image in the database for avatar display.

**Storage Notes**: The actual image file of the Microsoft avatar is stored in Cloudflare R2. This is image data that we actively process and store, unlike the processing method for user-defined avatars (only the URL is stored).

**Legal Basis**: Article 13, Paragraph 1 of the Personal Information Protection Law—Obtaining the individual's **explicit consent** (your consent to authorization on the Microsoft OAuth authorization page).

**Storage Period**: Linked to the account's lifespan. When the account is deleted, the avatar file stored on Cloudflare R2 and the URL record in the database are simultaneously deleted.

### 2.3 QR Code Login Functionality

**Collection Content**: When you log in using a QR code, we temporarily collect the **client IP address**, **browser User-Agent information**, and **QR code session token** to verify device identity and ensure login security.

**Collection Method**: Automatically collected during QR code generation and scanning. All information is stored encrypted. The session token is valid for only 3 minutes.

**Collection Purpose**: To enable convenient cross-device identity authentication, allowing you to quickly log in by scanning a QR code on your mobile phone from your computer.

**Legal Basis**: Article 13, Paragraph 2 of the Personal Information Protection Law—Necessary for concluding and performing contracts in which an individual is a party.

**Storage Period**: The QR code login session token is deleted immediately after use. Unused tokens automatically expire and are deleted after 3 minutes.

### 2.4 OAuth 2.0 Authorization Service

**Collection Content**: When you authorize a third-party application to use your website account, we will collect and store **basic information of the third-party application** (application name, description, callback URL) and **authorization scope record**.

**Collection Method**: Collected through your explicit authorization action on the authorization page.

**Collection Purpose**: To allow you to securely authorize trusted third-party applications to use your website account, and to record the authorization history for your management.

**Legal Basis**: Article 13, Paragraph 1 of the Personal Information Protection Law—Obtaining the individual's **explicit consent**.

**Storage Period**: Authorization records will be stored until you actively revoke the authorization or cancel your account.

### 2.5 Operation Log Recording

**Collection Content**: We automatically record the following user operation logs:
- **Account Management Operations**: Registration, Password Change, Username Change, Avatar Change, Account Deletion
- **Third-Party Account Binding**: Binding/Unbinding a Microsoft Account
- **OAuth Authorization Operations**: Authorizing Third-Party Applications, Revoking Authorization
- **Account Status Changes**: Banned/Unbanned (administrator operation)

Each log entry includes: **User UID**, **Operation Type**, **Operation Time**, **Operation Details**.

**Operation Details Explanation**: For operations involving information changes (such as changing username or avatar), the log records the values ​​before and after the change. For example, when changing a username, the old and new usernames will be recorded; when changing an avatar, the old and new avatar URLs will be recorded. This historical data is also your personal information and will be retained along with the logs for 6 months before being automatically deleted.

**Collection Method**: The system automatically records these operations when you perform them; no additional action from you is required.

**Purpose of Collection**:
1. **Security Audit**: Detect abnormal operational behavior and ensure account security.
2. **Troubleshooting**: Assist users in resolving account-related issues.
3. **Compliance Requirements**: Meet legal and regulatory requirements for the retention of operation logs.

**Legal Basis**: Article 13, Paragraph 2 of the Personal Information Protection Law—Necessary for the conclusion and performance of contracts in which an individual is a party.

**Retention Period**: Operation logs are retained for **6 months**, after which they are automatically deleted. **For security audit purposes, operation logs are retained for 6 months after account cancellation, after which they are automatically deleted.** This retention does not affect the immediate deletion of your account's core data (username, email, etc.) upon cancellation.

## 4. Data Storage, Protection, and Deletion Mechanisms

### 4.1 Storage Architecture and Duration

Your personal information is stored on our servers located **within Mainland China**, using a logically isolated database storage architecture.

| Data Type | Storage Location | Storage Duration | Expiration Deletion Mechanism |
|---------|---------|---------|------------|
| Username, Email, Password Hash | Main Database (Encrypted Tablespace) | Account Duration | Deleted immediately after account cancellation, automatically cleared within 30 days of backup |
| Registration Timestamp | Main Database | Account Duration | Deleted synchronously with account information |
| Avatar URL (User-defined) | Main Database | Account Duration | Deleted immediately after account cancellation |
| Microsoft Account Information | Main Database | Deleted immediately after account cancellation or unbinding | Deleted synchronously upon account cancellation |
| Microsoft Avatar Image | Cloudflare R2 | Deleted after account cancellation or unbinding | Delete image file after account cancellation or unbinding |
| QR Code Login Session | Main Database | 3 minutes | Deleted immediately after use, automatically cleared after expiration for unused sessions |
| OAuth Authorization Record | Main Database | Authorization Duration | Deleted immediately upon authorization revocation or account cancellation |
| Operation Log | Auxiliary Log Library | 6 Months | Automatically deleted upon expiration; retained for 6 months after account cancellation for security auditing, then automatically deleted |
| Backup Data | Off-site Backup Storage | 30 Days | Automatic Rotation Overwrite |

### 4.2 Data Security Technical Measures

1. **Transport Layer Security**: Encrypted transmission throughout using the **TLS 1.3** protocol provided by Cloudflare, with HSTS and Certificate Transparency (CT) logs enabled.
2. **Password Encryption**: Employs the **Argon2id** algorithm, with a memory parameter of 64MB, an iteration count of 1, and a parallelism of 2. Each password uses an independent random salt.
3. **Database Encryption**: Transparent Data Encryption (TDE) is enabled in the tablespace storing usernames and email addresses.
4. **Access Control**: Database access permissions follow the principle of least privilege, with only **core operations and maintenance personnel** having access rights.

We cannot control Cloudflare's data collection practices, and its processing activities are governed by its own [Privacy Policy](https://www.cloudflare.com/privacypolicy/).

When you use Microsoft account login or linking features, we obtain your user ID, display name, avatar image, and other information from Microsoft. Microsoft's data processing activities are governed by its own [Privacy Policy](https://www.microsoft.com/privacy/privacystatement).

### 5.3 Explanation of Cross-Border Data Transfer

Due to the technical characteristics of Cloudflare's (a US company) CDN service, data transmission paths may pass through server nodes located outside of China. However, our main database of personal information is stored on servers located in mainland China, and **we do not actively provide personal information to any third parties overseas.**

**Security Measures:**
1. **Encrypted Transmission:** All data transmission is encrypted using TLS to ensure that data is not stolen or tampered with during transmission.
2. **Minimization Principle:** Only the minimum data necessary for the service is transmitted.
