## 1. What is a Cookie?

A cookie is a small text file sent by a website server to your browser and stored on your device. They allow websites to recognize your device and remember certain information, such as your login status or language preferences, on subsequent visits.

---

## 2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies **strictly limited** to providing core service functionality and do not use them for user tracking, behavioral analysis, or advertising.

### 2.1 Cookie Classification

Based on their function and purpose, we classify cookies into the following two categories:

| Type | Description | Consent Required |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Required Cookie** | Essential for the normal operation of the service; rejection will result in some functions being unavailable | No |
| **Optional Cookie** | Used to enhance user experience; rejection does not affect core functions | Yes |

### 2.2 Cookie Consent Mechanism

When you first visit this website, a Cookie Consent banner will be displayed at the bottom of the page. You can choose:
- **Accept**: Allow us to set optional cookies
- **Reject**: Only retain required cookies; existing optional cookies will be automatically deleted

Your choice will be recorded in the `cookieConsent` cookie, valid for 365 days.

## 4. Cookie Security Attribute Description

We have configured corresponding security attributes for different types of cookies:

### 4.1 HttpOnly Attribute

| Cookie | HttpOnly | Description |
|--------|----------|------|
| `token`, `link_token` | Yes | Prevents JavaScript from reading, reducing the risk of XSS attacks stealing tokens |
| Other Cookies | No | Allows front-end JavaScript to read for corresponding functions |

### 4.2 SameSite Attribute

All cookies are set to `SameSite=Lax`, which means:
- Cookies will be sent in same-site requests
- Cookies are also sent when navigating to this site from an external website (allowed in GET requests)
- Cross-site POST requests or cross-site requests initiated by scripts will not carry cookies

This configuration ensures security without affecting the normal user navigation experience.

### 4.3 Secure Attribute

All cookies have the `Secure` attribute enabled to ensure that cookies are transmitted only over HTTPS encrypted connections, preventing man-in-the-middle attacks.

### 4.4 Path and Domain Attributes

- **Path**: All cookies have their Path set to `/`, meaning they are available site-wide.
- **Domain**: Set to `www.nebulastudios.top`. Cookies are only valid for the www subdomain and are isolated from other subdomains.

## 6. How to Manage Cookies

### 6.1 Through Our Website

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time by:
- Clear your browser data and revisit the website to display the banner again.
- Manually delete the `cookieConsent` cookie and refresh the page.

### 6.2 Through Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to view, delete, or block cookies. You can do the following through your browser settings:
- View stored cookies
- Delete all or specific cookies
- Block all cookies or only third-party cookies
- Set a allowlist for specific websites

**Please Note**: If you block necessary cookies, core functions of this service, such as login and verification code sending, will not function properly.

### 6.3 Common Browser Cookie Management Guidelines

| Browser | Management Path |
|--------|---------|
| Chrome | Settings → Privacy & Security → Third-Party Cookies |
| Firefox | Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Website Data |
| Safari | Preferences → Privacy → Cookies and Website Data |
| Edge | Settings → Cookies and Website Permissions → Cookies and Website Data |
